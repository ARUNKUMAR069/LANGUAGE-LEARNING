What is a Language

A language can be defined as a system of communication consisting of a set of sounds, symbols, and rules used by humans for expressing thoughts, ideas, feelings, and information. It encompasses spoken, written, and sometimes non-verbal forms of expression, allowing individuals to convey meaning and understand one another within a particular community or culture. Languages typically involve vocabulary (words), grammar (rules for constructing sentences), syntax (arrangement of words in sentences), and semantics (meaning conveyed by words and sentences). Language is fundamental to human interaction, culture, and cognition, serving as a means of communication, expression, and identity.
